About the Event

The annual flagship event, Section Student Congress '24, of IEEE Hyderabad Section's Student Activities Committee was held at the Tagore Auditorium, OUCE Student Branch, on 19th October 2024, with the theme "Semiconductors in AI Evolution."

This congress aimed to bring together student branches from different regions of the IEEE Hyderabad Section to foster collaboration, share knowledge about emerging technologies, network with professionals, and build connections to support their career development.

The event was hosted by Heramba Sai Ganesh and Mithuna, Student Network members, who ensured its smooth flow.

Key Takeaways from Sessions

Session 1: Semiconductors for AI - Edge Intelligence Perspective

Speaker: Mr. N. Venkatesh, Senior Director, Silicon Labs

  • Edge intelligence enables devices to manage data locally and offload heavy processing to cloud platforms like AWS.
  • Chip design involves analog and RF circuits, signal processing, embedded code, and microcontrollers working in unison.
  • Edge intelligence is more efficient, providing faster processing, enabling alarms without internet connectivity, and being more cost-effective.
  • Practical application: Fall detection devices use accelerometers to detect free-fall states through negative acceleration spikes.

Session 2: Industry-ready Requirements for RADAR and Sensor Engineering

Speaker: Dr. Madumita Chakravarti

  • RADAR technology evolved from World War II and remains highly relevant for air traffic control, remote sensing, traffic control, and defense applications.
  • As frequency increases, wavelength decreases, leading to smaller antennas with high potential for terahertz frequency applications.
  • Electronic warfare systems are now integrated with cyber systems, with success depending on system synergy.
  • SiGe and integrated CMOS technologies are growing rapidly, with flexible RF allowing cost-effective manufacturing.

Session 3: How Semiconductors are Powering AI Evolution

Speaker: Mr. Uma Desu

  • GenAI skills are in high demand, with companies like TCS offering 9 LPAs and Cognizant offering 12 LPAs for GenAI-related roles.
  • Nvidia's CEO highlighted a million-fold improvement in semiconductor efficiency over the last decade, enabling advanced chips that power tools like ChatGPT.
  • The Blackwell GPU can train up to 8 ChatGPTs simultaneously, with companies worldwide trying to acquire these chips.
  • Quantum computing is being used in delivery robots for route optimization, using qubits to provide 8 different scenarios for decision-making.
  • Companies are struggling to find candidates with GenAI skills, with only 1,000 students in Telugu states currently prepared for such roles.

Session 4: Powering the AI Revolution - Semiconductors in Energy Systems

Speaker: Dr. Murthy Balejapalli

  • Emerging technologies like SiC (silicon carbide) inverters and GaN (gallium nitride) applications are enhancing power electronics and making smart grids more efficient.
  • Smart meters are replacing traditional meters, playing a crucial role in demand response by continuously monitoring real-time data for efficient grid management.
  • IoT-based forecasting and real-time load prediction are essential for energy management, ensuring the grid can meet demand accurately.
  • AI-enabled semiconductor sensors can autonomously detect failures or abnormal conditions and report to the governor, with significant applications in heat dissipation management.
  • The Ministry of Power has launched 14 pilot projects to transition from traditional to advanced energy systems, demonstrating the potential of AI and semiconductors.

Event Impact

The flagship SSC '24 event served as a bridge between academia and industry, immersing participants in the latest advancements in semiconductors and AI. With practical insights and interactive activities, it strengthened student engagement and career preparedness.
